Vaibhav Sharma has over 16 years of experience across corporate, commercial, and litigation matters. He holds a Master of Laws degree from the Army Institute of Law, Mohali.

From 2013 to 2017, he served as Deputy Advocate General for the State of Punjab, representing the state in high-profile matters before the Supreme Court and the Punjab and Haryana High Court. His work in that role covered writs, taxation, criminal, and service law. From 2020 to 2022, he served as Standing Counsel for the Comptroller and Auditor General of India in Haryana. He is also an Empanelled Counsel for the Punjab State Agricultural Marketing Board.

His practice spans civil and criminal litigation, corporate disputes, taxation, and white-collar crime. A substantial portion of his work is in corporate law, where he advises on mergers and acquisitions, legal due diligence, deal structuring, and transaction documentation. He has represented stakeholders in insolvency and bankruptcy proceedings under the IBC and has handled cases of oppression and mismanagement before the National Company Law Tribunal. His exposure to a range of legal disciplines allows him to identify intersections between different areas of law that may affect a client's position.

He maintains an active interest in the intersection of law and emerging technologies, particularly artificial intelligence and its implications for the legal profession. He is an advocate for the adoption of legal technology to enhance efficiency and access to justice. In 2012, he was included in Forbes' list of top ten Power Influencers in Mobile worldwide.

He regularly participates in conferences and workshops on legal technology, innovation, and advanced corporate law. In his formative years, he distinguished himself in national and international moot court competitions.
